**Purpose**: Choose between two go-to-market strategies with clear timelines and resource requirements.

**Context**:
- **Team**: 3 cofounders (2 software engineers, 1 solutions engineer), currently working professionals
- **Sabbatical opportunity**: 1 cofounder has a month-long sabbatical in March 2026
- **Current status**: Business registered, ready to build
- **Decision needed**: Kickstarter-first vs Investment-first approach

## üéØ Decision Framework: Which Path Should You Choose?

### Option A: Kickstarter-First (Community-Funded Launch)
**Best if you want to:**
- ‚úÖ Validate market demand quickly with real customers
- ‚úÖ Build community and early adopters before scaling
- ‚úÖ Minimize dilution and maintain control
- ‚úÖ Generate revenue before raising investment
- ‚úÖ Prove traction to investors with real sales data

**Challenges:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Requires intense marketing effort in March-April
- ‚ö†Ô∏è 1 cofounder must go full-time in March (sabbatical)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Fundraising happens later (Sept) with less runway
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Slower initial scale (100-200 units first)

**Key Milestone**: Launch Kickstarter in April, raise S$70K-110K from 100-200 backers

---

### Option B: Investment-First (Capital-Backed Launch)
**Best if you want to:**
- ‚úÖ Secure runway and founder salaries upfront (S$250K-500K)
- ‚úÖ Build more robust product before public launch
- ‚úÖ Scale faster with capital for inventory and marketing
- ‚úÖ Reduce personal financial risk for cofounders
- ‚úÖ Hire support staff earlier

**Challenges:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Takes 2-3 months to close investment (Feb-Apr)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Requires strong pitch and investor network
- ‚ö†Ô∏è More dilution upfront (10-20% equity)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Less market validation before building
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Investor pressure and reporting requirements

**Key Milestone**: Close pre-seed round by April (S$250K-500K), launch DTC in June

## Choose Kickstarter-First if:
1. You want to validate market demand before committing full-time
2. You have strong community connections (Women Devs SG, parenting networks)
3. You're comfortable with slower initial growth (100-200 units first)
4. You want to minimize dilution and maintain control
5. You can leverage the March sabbatical for intense marketing push

## Choose Investment-First if:
1. You have strong investor connections and can close S$250K-500K by March
2. You want all 3 cofounders full-time by April (reduces personal financial risk)
3. You want to build a more robust product before public launch
4. You're comfortable with 10-20% dilution upfront
5. You want to scale faster with capital for inventory and marketing

## Hybrid Option: Kickstarter + Investment
- **Feb-Mar**: Run Kickstarter campaign in parallel with investor meetings
- **April**: Close both Kickstarter (S$70K-110K) AND pre-seed (S$250K-500K)
- **Benefit**: Market validation + capital for scale
- **Challenge**: Requires intense effort from all 3 cofounders in March-April
